Career

Early Years at Everton

Wayne Rooney made his debut for Everton at the age of 16, quickly making a name for himself with his natural talent and goal-scoring ability. His performances caught the attention of top clubs around the world.

Manchester United

In 2004, Rooney signed with Manchester United, where he would spend the majority of his career. During his time at United, he won numerous Premier League titles, the FA Cup, and the UEFA Champions League, among other honors.

Rooney's versatility allowed him to play in various attacking roles, and he became the club's all-time leading scorer, surpassing Sir Bobby Charlton's record.

Return to Everton and Later Career

Rooney returned to Everton in 2017 for a brief period before moving to Major League Soccer's D.C. United. He finished his playing career at Derby County, where he transitioned into a managerial role.
